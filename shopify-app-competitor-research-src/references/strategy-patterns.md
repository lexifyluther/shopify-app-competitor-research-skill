# Strategy Pattern Recognition Framework

Use this reference during Layer 3 analysis to identify the competitor's overall marketing strategy, business stage, and growth model.

---

## Growth Model Patterns

### Product-Led Growth (PLG)

**Core idea:** The product itself is the primary growth engine. Users try it, get value, tell others.

**Signals:**
- Generous free plan or long free trial (14-30 days)
- Self-serve onboarding — no demo required, no sales contact needed
- In-app viral loops: referral prompts, "powered by [app]" badges on merchant stores
- Low or no investment in outbound sales (no SDR team on LinkedIn)
- App store listing emphasizes ease of setup ("install in 2 minutes")
- High review count relative to marketing investment (organic word-of-mouth)
- Feature comparison focused on UX/simplicity rather than enterprise features

**Budget profile:** Low marketing spend, high product/engineering spend.

---

### Content/SEO-Led Growth

**Core idea:** Attract merchants through valuable content that ranks in search, building trust over time.

**Signals:**
- Blog with 50+ posts, regular publishing schedule (2-8 posts/month)
- Content organized in topic clusters (hub-and-spoke model)
- Top-funnel content targeting merchant pain points ("how to increase AOV", "best upsell strategies")
- Bottom-funnel content targeting comparison/alternative keywords ("[competitor] alternative", "best [category] app")
- Significant organic traffic (check with Ubersuggest)
- Downloadable resources: playbooks, guides, templates, calculators (lead magnets)
- Webinar program (monthly or quarterly)
- Guest posting on ecommerce blogs

**Budget profile:** Medium-high content production cost, low paid acquisition, slow but compounding returns.

---

### Paid Acquisition Heavy

**Core idea:** Buy traffic and installs through advertising, optimized for ROAS.

**Signals:**
- Many active ads across Google, Meta, LinkedIn (10+ active at once)
- Multiple ad creative variations = active A/B testing
- Dedicated landing pages (not just homepage) for different campaigns
- Retargeting pixels present on website
- Keyword bidding on competitor names ("better than [competitor]")
- App store keyword ads (if available in the user's market)
- Fast creative rotation = optimizing actively

**Budget profile:** High and ongoing. Sustainable only if LTV > CAC with margin. Watch for signs of scaling back (reduced ad count over time = possibly not profitable).

---

### Partnership/Channel-Led Growth

**Core idea:** Grow through distribution partners — agencies, integrations, Shopify itself.

**Signals:**
- Formal agency/partner program with dedicated landing page
- Partner tiers with commission or revenue share
- Listed on Shoffi.app, PartnerStack, or has affiliate program
- Multiple technology integrations prominently featured
- Co-marketing content with partners (joint webinars, co-authored blog posts)
- Presence in Shopify Partner Directory, Shopify Plus Certified
- Agency testimonials on website
- Team includes "Partner Manager" or "Channel Sales" roles (check LinkedIn)

**Budget profile:** Revenue share reduces per-unit economics but acquisition is highly scalable. Often combined with PLG or Content-Led for the non-partner channel.

---

### Community-Led Growth

**Core idea:** Build a community of users and potential users who help each other, creating a moat.

**Signals:**
- Active Discord, Slack, or Facebook Group with hundreds/thousands of members
- Dedicated community manager (check team page, LinkedIn)
- Community used for feature requests, beta testing, peer support
- User-generated content: tutorials, templates, tips shared by community members
- Community events: AMAs, challenges, meetups
- Brand identity that's personal/approachable rather than corporate
- Founder actively participates in community

**Budget profile:** Low direct cost but high time investment. Compounds over time.

---

### Outbound/Sales-Led Growth

**Core idea:** Proactively reach out to merchants through sales teams.

**Signals:**
- SDR/BDR team visible on LinkedIn (company has 3+ people with sales titles)
- LinkedIn outreach activity (founder or sales team actively posting, connecting with merchants)
- Demo-focused CTAs ("Book a demo", "Talk to sales") rather than "Install free"
- Enterprise pricing tier (custom/contact us)
- Targeting Shopify Plus merchants specifically
- Cold email campaigns (check if they use tools like Apollo, Outreach)
- Limited investment in SEO/content — acquisition is primarily outbound

**Budget profile:** High (salaries + tools), but can close high-value deals.

---

### Hybrid (Most Common)

Most mature Shopify apps use a combination. Identify the **primary** model (where most installs come from) and **secondary** channels. Common hybrids:

- **PLG + Content**: free plan drives installs, blog/SEO drives awareness (very common for mid-size apps)
- **PLG + Partnership**: free plan + agency program (common growth path: start PLG, add partnerships at scale)
- **Content + Paid**: SEO for long-term, paid for immediate pipeline (common during growth stage)
- **Partnership + Sales**: agency partners for volume, direct sales for enterprise (common for Shopify Plus-focused apps)

## Business Stage Assessment

### Pre-launch / Beta
- Not yet public on App Store, or very recently launched (<1 month)
- Waitlist, beta feedback requests
- Marketing: landing page, founder personal brand, early community building
- Budget: minimal, founder-led everything

### Go-to-Market (0-12 months post-launch)
- Few reviews (<50), still finding product-market fit
- Experimenting across channels — messaging not yet consistent
- Founder-led marketing (CEO writes blog posts, does demos, answers every review)
- May pivot messaging/positioning multiple times
- Budget: growing but unfocused

### Growth (1-3 years, finding what works)
- Reviews growing steadily (50-500+)
- Doubling down on channels that work, cutting what doesn't
- Hiring first marketing team members
- Starting agency/partner relationships
- Budget: increasing, becoming more focused

### Scale (3+ years, known playbook)
- Thousands of reviews, established brand in category
- Multi-channel machine: content + paid + partnerships running simultaneously
- Enterprise/Plus features, international expansion
- Defensive marketing: comparison pages, competitor keyword bidding
- Dedicated teams per channel
- Budget: significant, optimized

### Mature / Defend
- Market leader or top 3 in category
- Growth slowing, focus shifts to retention and ARPU expansion
- Acquiring smaller competitors/apps
- Heavy investment in customer success as marketing
- May see budget reallocation from acquisition to retention
- Signs: fewer new feature launches, more stability/reliability messaging

---

## Stage Transition Signals

Watch for these signals that indicate an app is moving between stages:

| From → To | Signal |
|---|---|
| Go-to-Market → Growth | Messaging stabilizes, one channel clearly getting investment, hiring marketing roles |
| Growth → Scale | Launching partner program, adding enterprise tier, multi-language support |
| Scale → Mature | Competitor comparison pages appear, acquisition rumors, retention-focused features |
| Any stage → Decline | Review sentiment worsening, social activity dropping, ads stopping, team shrinking (LinkedIn) |
