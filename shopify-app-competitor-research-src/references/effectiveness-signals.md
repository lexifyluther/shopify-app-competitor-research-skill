# Effectiveness Signal Framework

Use this reference to evaluate which channels and messages are working vs failing for the competitor. The key principle: you cannot see their dashboards, but you CAN read behavioral signals that reveal what's working.

---

## Channel Effectiveness Signals

### Signals That a Channel Is Working (Positive)

**Sustained investment over time:**
- Channel has been active for 6+ months with consistent or increasing effort
- Why this matters: rational companies cut channels that don't work. If they keep investing, it's likely returning results.

**Increasing investment:**
- Ad count growing month over month
- Blog publishing frequency increasing
- Social posting becoming more frequent
- New hires for that channel (check LinkedIn for recent "joined" dates)
- Why: scaling what works is the natural response to positive ROI.

**Format repetition:**
- Same ad format/template reused with variations (e.g., always using customer testimonial + metric in ads)
- Same blog content template repeated (e.g., "How [Customer] achieved X with [App]")
- Why: when a format converts, marketers replicate it.

**Long-running ads:**
- An ad that's been active for 3+ months is almost certainly profitable
- Platforms optimize delivery toward working ads — survival = performance
- Multiple long-running ads in different markets = scaling a winner

**Cross-channel reinforcement:**
- Same message/angle appears on multiple channels simultaneously
- Blog post → social promotion → ad → email — coordinated push
- Why: they've found a winning message and are amplifying it everywhere

**Community engagement quality:**
- Active community with genuine peer-to-peer help (not just company posts)
- Users defending the app in Reddit/forum threads = strong word-of-mouth
- Why: authentic community engagement can't be faked and indicates product satisfaction

---

### Signals That a Channel Is Failing (Negative)

**Abandoned or dormant channel:**
- Blog hasn't been updated in 2+ months
- Social account posts sporadically (1-2 times/month or less)
- Community/Discord exists but has very low activity
- YouTube channel with 3 videos from 8 months ago
- Why: they tried it, it didn't work, they stopped investing.

**Rapid messaging changes:**
- Value proposition changes every few weeks across ads/content
- Rebrand or repositioning mid-stream
- Why: they haven't found the message that resonates. Still searching.

**Low engagement relative to effort:**
- Posting 3x/week on LinkedIn with 2-5 likes per post
- Blog posts with zero comments and low estimated traffic
- YouTube videos with <100 views despite regular posting
- Why: investment without return. Channel may not match their audience.

**Presence without conversion path:**
- Social media active but no links to app, no CTAs, no lead capture
- Blog posts that don't mention the product or link to install
- Why: "marketing theater" — doing it for appearances, not results.

**Ads with short lifespan:**
- Many ads created and stopped within 1-2 weeks
- High creative turnover = nothing is converting well enough to keep
- Why: constant testing with no winners found yet.

## Message Effectiveness Signals

### How to Identify Winning Messages

**Engagement comparison method:**
Compare engagement (likes, comments, shares, views) across different message angles from the SAME account. This controls for audience size and platform algorithm.

Example analysis:
- Post about "save time on [task]" → 500 likes, 40 comments
- Post about "increase revenue by X%" → 50 likes, 3 comments
- Post about "easy setup, no coding" → 200 likes, 15 comments
- → **"save time" resonates most** with their audience

**Repetition = winner:**
- If the same message angle appears in ads, blog titles, app store description, and social posts, it's their winning message
- Track which phrases/claims appear most frequently across ALL channels

**Ad longevity = conversion:**
- Which ad copy has been running longest? That's likely their best-converting message.
- Compare: testimonial ads vs feature ads vs pain-point ads — which type survives?

**Content performance by topic:**
- Which blog posts have the most estimated organic traffic?
- Which YouTube videos have the most views?
- Which social posts got the most engagement?
- Map these back to the message/angle used → reveals what the MARKET (not just the company) responds to.

## Budget Estimation Heuristics

These are rough estimates. Always state they are estimates and show your reasoning.

### Paid Ads
- Google Ads: count active ads × estimated CPC ($2-8 for Shopify/ecommerce keywords) × estimated daily clicks
- Meta Ads: broader range, $1-5 CPC for B2B/SaaS targeting Shopify merchants
- LinkedIn: expensive, $5-15 CPC. Even a few ads = meaningful budget
- TikTok: cheaper reach but less targeted, $0.50-3 CPC

### Content
- Blog: $200-1000/post (outsourced) or 1 full-time content person ($5-8k/month)
- Webinar: $1-5k per event (platform + promotion + speaker time)
- Podcast: $500-2000/month (production + hosting)
- Video: $500-5000/video depending on production quality

### Community
- Community manager: $4-8k/month (full-time) or $1-2k/month (part-time/contractor)
- Platform costs: most are free (Discord, Facebook Groups)
- Events/meetups: $500-3000/event

### Partnerships
- Agency partner manager: $6-10k/month (salary)
- Partner commission: typically 15-30% recurring for first 12 months
- Co-marketing: $1-5k/campaign

### Total Monthly Budget Estimation
Sum up the estimated costs across active channels:
- **Minimal** (<$5k/month): founder-led, 1-2 channels, mostly organic
- **Growing** ($5-20k/month): 1-2 dedicated people, paid + organic mix
- **Significant** ($20-100k/month): team of 3-5, multi-channel active campaigns
- **Enterprise** ($100k+/month): full marketing team, heavy paid, agency partnerships, events

## Competitive Advantage Assessment

After analyzing all channels and effectiveness:

### What constitutes a marketing moat?

**Strong moats (hard to replicate):**
- Large, active community (takes years to build)
- 1,000+ positive reviews with high rating (can't be bought)
- Strong organic search rankings for key terms (takes 12-24 months)
- Deep agency/partner network (relationship-based, takes years)
- Being recommended by AI chatbots (depends on widespread mentions and quality)

**Moderate moats:**
- Extensive content library (can be replicated in 6-12 months)
- Active affiliate network (can be built with right incentives)
- Social media following (can be grown but takes time)

**Weak moats (easily replicated):**
- Paid ads (anyone can buy them)
- App store listing optimization (can be improved quickly)
- Pricing strategy (can be matched)

### Identifying gaps/opportunities:

Look for channels where the competitor has:
- NO presence (complete gap — opportunity to own)
- WEAK presence (low engagement, dormant — opportunity to outperform)
- EMERGING presence (just started — can still compete)

And channels where they have:
- DOMINANT presence (strong moat — consider avoiding direct competition, find alternative angle)
